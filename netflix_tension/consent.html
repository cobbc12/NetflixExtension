Video Survey

We are asking you to be in a research study. This form gives you information to help you decide whether or not to be in the study. Being in the study is voluntary. Please read this carefully. You may ask any questions about the study. Then you can decide whether or not you want to be in the study.

PURPOSE OF THE STUDY

We are investigating how people perceive computer security and privacy scenarios in media such as TV shows, movies, and online videos. We are interested in how media influences people's security and privacy preferences and practices, as well as how realistic people think computer security and privacy is portrayed.

STUDY PROCEDURES

We will use the extension you just installed to monitor your video consumption across the Internet. This includes what you are watching, when you watch it, how much of it you watch, and what parts of it you watch. Through the extension, we will occasionally prompt you with questions, such as whether you think the scene you just watched was realistic, if you have experienced similar scenarios, or your opinions about certain security and privacy practices. While you can answer these questions with a mouse and/or keyboard, you may also consent to verbally answering certain questions (for example, if you are watching on a TV and do not want to use your phone or laptop to answer). When you consent to verbally answer questions, we will use your browser's speech recognition API to convert your speech into text. While your voice is temporarily recorded, we will only receive your responses in text form. You may decline to answer any question at any time without penalty. You may also temporarily disable or uninstall this extension to avoid any data being collected. No data will be collected in your browser's Incognito mode. We may also collect information about your usage of the extension (such as how often you use it and when you consented to participate in this story) and information about your computer and/or phone.

RISKS, STRESS, OR DISCOMFORT

We will be collecting your video consumption history across the Internet. You may be uncomfortable or embarassed if someone discovers your viewing history. While all data is collected anonymously, it may be possible to deanonymize the data, particularly if you publicly review videos or movies you watch. While the data we collect is transmitted, processed, and stored securely, there is a risk that these systems may be breached, in which case your data may be exposed. While we believe our extension is bug-free, there is always a risk that this extension may have flaws or conflict with other software you have installed, potentially affecting the reliability and/or security of your phone or computer. Like any other extention, there is a small risk that a malicious update to this extension is pushed to your computer by hackers. We use hardware-based two-factor authentication to secure our accounts used to publish this extension. Finally, if your video consumption history becomes subject to civil or criminal legal proceedings, we may be compelled to provide your data, especially if the unique ID we assign your computer is obtained. Your history will be retained by us even if you delete your browser history.

BENEFITS OF THE STUDY

While we do not expect you to personally benefit from participating in this study, your participation may help us understand how the general public perceives computer security and privacy in the media, and may inform better system designs to account for common misconceptions, more effective ways of encouraging the adoption of good computer security and privacy practices, and more accurate portrayal of security and privacy in the media.
